What are Taylor and Maclaurin Series?.

I

Taylor Polynomials is a way to approximate non-polynomial functions with polynomials because polynomials are often easier to deal with. Higher degrees of the approximating polynomials correponds to a higer accuracy of the approximation. While a constant term approxiation can only make sure that the two graphs intersect a point c, which is the point that the approximating polynomial is centered at, the ax+b approximation can secure a identical first derivative at c and the 2nd degree polynomial can secure the 2nd derivative and so on.

Definition of the Taylor Polynomial: If f is degree n and centered at c:

$P_n(x) = f(c) + f'(c)(x-c) + (f''(c)/2!)(x-c)^2+...+(f^n(c)/n!)(x-c)^n$

If c = 0, then the Taylor polynomial centered/about 0 is known as a nth degree Maclaurin polynomial for f:

$P_n(x) = f(0) + f'(0)(x) + (f''(0)/2!)(x)^2+...+(f^n(0)/n!)(x)^n$

We use the reaminder of Taylor Polynomials to measure the accuracy of an approximating function:

$f(x) = P_n(x) + R_n(x)$

The absolute value of the remainder is known as the error associated with the appoximation:

$Error = |f(x)-P_n(x)| = |R_n(x)|$

Lagrage Remainder(Part of Taylor's Theorem):

$R_n(x) = ((f^{n+1}(c))/(n+1)!)(x-a)^{n+1}$

Where for some value between a and x:

$c \in (a, x)$

A power series is a method of exact approximation of a function. It is a variable series, meaning that the series, from its value to its convergence/divergence state, depends on the value of the variable. If x is a variable, then below is a power series centered/about at c:

$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(x-c)^n = a_0+a_1(x-c)+...+a_n(x-c)^n+...$

A power series can converge at a radius or interval(two times radius) of convergence. The interval of convergence (for a series centered at a) is either (a-r, a+r), [a-r, a+r], ...etc. The taylor series is defined as a type of power series.

A Taylor series is a type of power series. A Taylor series about x=a is defined as:

$f(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x-a) + (f''(a)/2!)(x-a)^2 + ... + (f^n(a)/n!)(x-a)^n + ...$
